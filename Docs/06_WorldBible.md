> Canonical lore, world design, zone-by-zone notes, quest outlines, and NPC roster.

## 1. The premise (the elevator pitch)

Keldran is a fractured continent. Long ago the **Seven-Throne Dominion** united seven
peoples under the **Crowns of Concord** — seven artifact-crowns whose shared resonance
held the land's wild magic in balance. A cataclysm called the **Sundering** shattered the
Crowns into countless **Shards** and tore open **Rifts**: unstable tears that bleed into
pocket-worlds with their own skies, seasons, and dangers.

From the ruin rose the **Hollow Legion**, commanded by the **Hollow King** — once a
Crown-keeper named **Corvane Ashentide**, now hollowed: a man emptied of self and filled
with the Sundering's silence. The Legion harvests Shards to widen the Rifts and unmake what
remains of the Dominion.

Players are **Shardbearers**: rare individuals who can touch a Shard without being hollowed.
They gather Shards, and the great question of the age — *should the Dominion be rebuilt, or
should the peoples stay free?* — splits the survivors into two factions who nonetheless must
face the Legion together.

**Tone:** realistic high-fantasy. Grounded, weathered, lived-in. Magic is awe-inspiring but
costly. No camp, no winking parody. Think weathered stone, real weather, tired heroes.

## 2. The Sundering — what actually happened (timeline)

- **The Concord Era (before year 0):** Seven peoples, seven Crowns, one Dominion centered on
  the city of **Lumengarde**. Peace held for ~600 years.
- **Year 0 — The Sundering:** An attempt to forge an *eighth* Crown (to bind a newly
  discovered eighth people, the **Ashborn**, by force) overloaded the Concord. The Crowns
  detonated. Six keepers died; the seventh, Corvane Ashentide, survived hollowed.
- **Years 0–40 — The Long Quiet:** Rifts spread. The Dominion's institutions collapsed into
  city-states and frontier holds. Two philosophies hardened into the present factions.
- **Year 47 (now / game present):** The Hollow Legion has begun *organized* Shard-harvesting.
  Shardbearers are being recruited by both factions. This is where the player enters.

Dates in-world are written **"Y47"** (47 years After Sundering). Use absolute Y-dates in all
generated lore so nothing drifts.

## 3. Factions

### Covenant of the Seven Crowns (Order / Restoration)
Believe the Sundering proved that unchecked freedom invites catastrophe, and only a rebuilt
Concord can hold the Rifts closed. Disciplined, archival, duty-bound. Seated in Lumengarde's
old institutions. Colors: deep blue and pale gold. Emblem: seven interlocked rings.
- **Leader:** **High Keeper Selene Varresi**, an Aelin archivist who survived the Long Quiet.
- **Stance:** collect Shards to *reforge* the Crowns. Risk: concentration of power.

### Free Pact of Ash (Frontier / Freedom)
Believe the Crowns were always a leash, and the Sundering freed the peoples to govern
themselves. Pragmatic, mobile, self-reliant. Seated in the frontier holds. Colors: rust-red
and ash-grey. Emblem: an open hand over a flame.
- **Leader:** **Pactmother Brannoch Dume**, a Grunvaldi forge-captain.
- **Stance:** collect Shards to *neutralize* them (break their pull) rather than reforge.
  Risk: leaving the Rifts permanently unstable.

### The Hollow Legion (shared PvE threat — not playable)
Hollowed soldiers, Rift-warped beasts, and Shard-engines. They do not negotiate. Led by the
**Hollow King, Corvane Ashentide**. Colors: bleached bone-white and void-black. Emblem: a
cracked crown with nothing inside the band.

## 6. L1 — Vael's Rest (the vertical slice zone, fully designed)

**Premise:** Vael's Rest is a Covenant waystation village in temperate woodland at the edge of
a minor Rift. A Shard-tremor has stirred the local wildlife and drawn a small Hollow scouting
party. The player, a newly-arrived Shardbearer, helps the village hold while recovering a
Shard fragment from the Rift's edge.

**Layout (single streamed World Partition map, ~1 km²):**
- **The Waystation** (hub): inn, notice board, vendor, quest-givers. Safe (no hostiles).
- **Thornwood Path:** forest road; low-level beasts (**Bramblebacks**).
- **Hollow's Toe:** a small Rift scar where Hollow scouts (**Hollow Stragglers**) have camped.
- **The Quiet Glade:** a clearing with the mini-boss (**Warden of the Toe**, a hollowed beast).

**Atmosphere:** dappled light, real volumetric fog at dawn, birdsong ambient that goes silent
near Hollow's Toe (audio storytelling). Coherence profile: overcast-to-golden temperate.

| Mob | Where | Behavior | Level | Loot focus |
|-----|-------|----------|-------|------------|
| **Brambleback** | Thornwood Path | passive→aggressive on approach; melee charge | 2–3 | hides, common drops |
| **Mire Skitter** | wet edges of path | aggressive swarmer; flees at low HP | 2–4 | reagents |
| **Hollow Straggler** | Hollow's Toe | aggressive; ranged void-bolt + melee | 3–5 | Shard dust, gear |
| **Warden of the Toe** (mini-boss) | Quiet Glade | telegraphed slam + adds | 5 (elite) | quest Shard fragment, blue item |

### 6.2 Slice quests (3–5, covering all objective types)
1. **"Boots on the Path"** *(reach)* — Quest-giver: **Serjant Halek Orne**. Travel from the
   Waystation to the Thornwood trailhead. Teaches movement/camera. Reward: starter potion x3.
2. **"Thin the Thorns"** *(kill)* — Halek Orne. Kill 6 Bramblebacks. Teaches combat/targeting.
   Reward: a common weapon upgrade (equip tutorial).
3. **"Salvage the Skitters"** *(collect)* — Vendor/herbalist **Mother Wend**. Collect 5
   *Skitter Glands* from Mire Skitters. Teaches loot/inventory. Reward: coin + a trinket.
4. **"The Hollow's Toe"** *(kill + reach)* — **Keeper-Initiate Talia Vond**. Clear 4 Hollow
   Stragglers and reach the Rift scar. Teaches threat/positioning. Reward: blue accessory.
5. **"Warden of the Toe"** *(boss + collect)* — Talia Vond. Defeat the mini-boss, recover the
   **Shard Fragment of Vael**, return it. Teaches the full loop. Reward: blue weapon + the
   slice's "you did it" moment (Shard recovered).

**Quest chain order:** 1 → 2 → 3 (3 parallel to 2) → 4 → 5. This is the M2 Gauntlet script's
content spine.

## 7. The Shard system (core fantasy)

- **Shards** are fragments of the broken Crowns. Touching one hollows ordinary people; only
  **Shardbearers** resist. Shards are the high-tier currency/progression hook (Phase 3+).
- In the slice, the **Shard Fragment of Vael** is the quest macguffin — a single named drop
  that ends the slice story. It is *not* yet a currency in M2 (that's M5 economy work).
- **Rifts** justify the biome variety (each Rift-world looks alien) and future fast-travel.

## 8. NPC dialogue & the "living NPC" demo

Most NPCs use **authored** bark pools + quest text (generated build-time via OpenRouter,
cached). **Innkeeper Doran Vale** is the single runtime "living NPC" demo: free-tier
OpenRouter model, per-NPC persona, world-bible context injection, guardrails, and an
**authored fallback line** when the service times out or hits quota. The client never calls
the LLM — it always goes through the backend NPC Dialogue Service (see `02_NetworkDesign.md`,
`03_AIContentPipeline.md`).

Persona seed (Doran Vale): *"A tired, kind frontier innkeeper in Vael's Rest. Speaks plainly,
deflects danger with dry humor, knows local gossip, never breaks setting, never mentions the
real world. Keeps replies under 3 sentences."*

## 9. Naming & style guide (keeps generated content original + consistent)

To stay distinct from Tolkien/Blizzard and internally coherent, generated names should follow
these patterns:
- **Aelin:** soft, vowel-led, elision (Selene, Talia, Vael, Lumen-). 
- **Grunvaldi:** hard consonants, Norse-ish but original (Brannoch, Dume, Grunvald, -hold).
- **Bryn / beastkin:** short, animal-adjacent but not literal (Bryn, Kell, Vesh).
- **Freeborn:** plain frontier names (Halek, Doran, Orne, Vale).
- **Ashborn:** ember/ash motifs (Ashentide, Ember-, Cinder-, Corvane).
- **Claykin:** earthy, doubled syllables (Wend, Pom, Marlow).
- **Places:** evocative compounds (Vael's Rest, Emberhold, Haelfrost, Cindreach, Verdance).
- **The Legion/Hollow:** negation/absence words (Hollow, Quiet, Sundered, Riven).

**Banned (hard):** any name, term, or iconography traceable to Tolkien or Blizzard/WoW
(no orc/elf-spelled-the-same proper nouns, no "Azeroth/Middle-earth"-style coinages, no
copied class/zone/spell names). When in doubt, invent and log the new term here.

**Glossary (canonical terms):** Sundering, Crowns of Concord, Shard, Shardbearer, Rift,
Hollow Legion, Hollow King, Dominion, Covenant of the Seven Crowns, Free Pact of Ash, Y47.
